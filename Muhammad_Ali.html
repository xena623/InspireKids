About Muhammad Ali's Story

Muhammad Ali's story is one of a boxer, activist, and humanitarian who overcame many challenges to become a champion and a symbol of the civil rights movement. Born Cassius Marcellus Clay Jr. in Louisville, Kentucky in 1942, Ali began boxing at age 12 and won a gold medal in the 1960 Summer Olympics. Ali won the world heavyweight championship three times, the first boxer to do so. He was known for his "rope-a-dope" technique and clever rhymes. Ali became a prominent figure in the civil rights movement and was known for his social work and charity. He was named a UN Messenger of Peace in 1998. He died on June 3 2016.
