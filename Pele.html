About Pelé's Story

Born Edson Arantes do Nascimento in 1940, Pelé grew up in poverty in the slums of São Paulo, Brazil. He taught himself to play soccer and dropped out of school to focus on the sport. At age 15, Pelé joined Santos Football Club and became the club's top scorer the following year. He was selected for the Brazil national team at age 16. Pelé led Brazil to three World Cup victories, in 1958, 1962, and 1970. At age 17, he became the youngest player to win a World Cup.
