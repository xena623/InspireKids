About Carl Sagan's Story

Carl Sagan, born in 1934 in Brooklyn, New York, grew up fascinated by the stars. His parents encouraged his curiosity, and he pursued studies in astronomy and astrophysics at the University of Chicago. Sagan made significant contributions to our understanding of planetary atmospheres and was an early advocate for the search for extraterrestrial life. Beyond his scientific work, Sagan became a household name through his TV series Cosmos: A Personal Voyage.He had a gift for making science accessible and inspiring, reminding humanity of its place in the vast universe.
